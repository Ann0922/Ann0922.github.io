In this paper, we explore a new AI-assisted fashion design problem for helping designers rapidly visualize an original garment, which can be effectively utilized by fashion design systems and machines to speed-up the design process. Given a guided pose along with texture patches of tops and bottoms, our goal is to automatically generate a series of fashion designs with various clothed models from different views. This would allow fashion designers to use automated tools to get inspiration and augment their creativity since the texture reveals the most important attributes of the cloth like patterns, colours, and prints while the pose simulates different viewports. Our work can even allow ordinary people to participate in fashion design, thus it greatly reduces the difficulty for user customization as the traditional process of fashion design requires an extensive amount of knowledge in creation and production of garments.

Data

Fashion-Gen contains 293,008 images of fashion items, in which each item is photographed from a variety of angles.
