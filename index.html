Pose and Texture Guided Multi-View Fashion Design Synthesis

In this paper, we explore a new AI-assisted fashion design problem for helping designers rapidly visualize an original garment, which can be effectively utilized by fashion design systems and machines to speed-up the design process. As illustrated in Figure 1, given a guided pose along with texture patches of tops and bottoms, our goal is to automatically generate a series of fashion designs with various clothed models from different views. This would allow fashion designers to use automated tools to get inspiration and augment their creativity since the texture reveals the most important attributes of the cloth like patterns, colours, and prints while the pose simulates different viewports.

Data

We evaluate our proposed PLAN on the Fashion-Gen dataset because other fashion datasets (e.g., DeepFashion, Fashionist, Fashionpedia) lack diversified clothing textures or multi-view images. Fashion-Gen contains 293,008 images of resolution 256Ã—256, in which each fashion item is photographed from a variety of angles. We select four dominant clothing categories (i.e. dresses, shirts, sweaters and tops) from 48 main fashion categories of the Fashion-Gen dataset for evaluation. On the other hand, we select front view, side view and back view as the view set since these three views are the most representative ones in the Fashion-Gen dataset. In total, we obtain 12,415 triplets, where each triplet contains three views of the same clothing item. We randomly select 10,416 triplets as our training set and select 1,999 triplets for testing.
